Mastering UTM Parameters

UTM (Urchin Tracking Module) parameters are tags added to the end of a URL. When someone clicks a link with these tags, they are sent back to your analytics platform (like Google Analytics), giving you precise data on where your traffic is coming from.

Using UTMs allows you to answer questions like: "Which marketing email drove the most sales?" or "Is my LinkedIn strategy working better than Facebook?"

anatomy of a link

https://example.com/page?utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=summer_sale

The 5 Parameters

🏷️ Source (utm_source)

Required. The origin of your traffic.

Ex: google, newsletter, linkedin

📡 Medium (utm_medium)

Required. The marketing medium.

Ex: cpc, email, social, referral

📣 Campaign (utm_campaign)

Required. The specific campaign name.

Ex: summer_sale, onboarding_v2

🔤 Term (utm_term)

Optional. Used for paid search keywords.

Ex: running+shoes, marketing+software

📝 Content (utm_content)

Optional. Differentiates similar content or links.

Ex: logo_link, text_link, cta_button

✅ Best Practices

  • Lowercase Only: GA is case sensitive. Email and email show as two different mediums. Always use lowercase.
  • Use Dashes/Underscores: Avoid spaces. Link Booster handles this, but aiming for summer_sale is safer than summer sale.
  • Be Consistent: Create a naming convention (e.g., always use facebook not fb) and stick to it.

❌ Common Mistakes

  • Internal Linking: NEVER use UTMs on links between pages of your own website. It restarts the session and messes up data.
  • PII Data: Never put names, emails, or phone numbers in UTM parameters. It violates privacy policies of tools like Google Analytics.

Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 10, 2026

Link Booster ("we", "our", or "us") respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy describes how we handle your data when you use the Link Booster Chrome Extension.

1. Data Collection

We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to our own servers.

All data generated by the extension (such as your link history, presets, and settings) is stored locally on your device using Chrome's storage.local API. This data never leaves your browser unless you explicitly choose to share it.

2. Third-Party Services

To provide specific features, we interact with the following third-party services:

  • TinyURL: When you use the "Shorten Link" feature, the URL you wish to shorten is sent to the TinyURL API (tinyurl.com). This is governed by TinyURL's Privacy Policy.

3. Permissions

The extension requests the following permissions for functionality only:

  • contextMenus: To allow you to right-click links and modify them.
  • scripting & activeTab: To display the QR code overlay and Toast notifications on the current page.
  • storage: To save your preferences and local history.

4. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page.

5. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us via the Chrome Web Store support tab.
